Crispy Fried Chicken

Description

Crispy fried chicken is a dish renowned for its harmonious balance of succulent, tender meat encased in a golden, crunchy exterior. The key to achieving this irresistible texture lies in the marriage of a well-seasoned marinade and a perfectly formulated batter. The chicken, typically bone-in and skin-on, is first immersed in a rich buttermilk mixture, which not only tenderizes the meat but also imparts a subtle tang that enhances the overall flavor profile. This marinade, infused with a blend of aromatic spices, infuses the chicken with deep, complex flavors that will develop over time, especially with an extended marination period. The buttermilk serves as the perfect adhesive for the subsequent flour coating, which, when combined with a touch of baking powder, ensures a delicate yet crisp crust.

The high heat of the oil transforms the flour dredge into a crisp, golden-brown shell, while the chicken inside remains moist and tender. The use of skin-on pieces helps retain the juiciness of the meat, while the skin itself crisps up beautifully, contributing both flavor and texture. With a balance of seasoning in the batter and marinade, the dish becomes a symphony of flavors, where each bite offers a satisfying contrast between the crunch of the coating and the tender, flavorful meat. The end result is a perfect equilibrium of texture, seasoning, and tenderness that defines the quintessential experience of crispy fried chicken.

Ingredients

  • 1 (4 pound) chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour for coating
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 quarts vegetable oil for frying

Steps

  1. Take your cut up chicken pieces and skin them if you prefer.
  2. Put the flour in a large plastic bag (let the amount of chicken you are cooking dictate the amount of flour you use). Season the flour with paprika, salt and pepper to taste (paprika helps to brown the chicken).
  3. Dip chicken pieces in buttermilk then, a few at a time, put them in the bag with the flour, seal the bag and shake to coat well.
  4. Place the coated chicken on a cookie sheet or tray, and cover with a clean dish towel or waxed paper. LET SIT UNTIL THE FLOUR IS OF A PASTE-LIKE CONSISTENCY. THIS IS CRUCIAL!
  5. Fill a large skillet (cast iron is best) about 1/3 to 1/2 full with vegetable oil. Heat until VERY hot.
  6. Put in as many chicken pieces as the skillet can hold. Brown the chicken in HOT oil on both sides.
  7. When browned, reduce heat and cover skillet; let cook for 30 minutes (the chicken will be cooked through but not crispy). Remove cover, raise heat again, and continue to fry until crispy.
  8. Drain the fried chicken on paper towels. Depending on how much chicken you have, you may have to fry in a few shifts. Keep the finished chicken in a slightly warm oven while preparing the rest.
